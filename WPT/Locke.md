# John Locke

---

## Intro

- Known as **Father of Liberalism**
- Gave theory of **Natural Rights**, **Government by Consent**, **Toleration**

---

## Context

- Witnessed **Glorious Revolution (1688)**
- Peaceful transfer of power from monarchy to parliament

---

## Critique of Filmer

- Filmer: Divine Right Theory → king absolute, state by God
- Locke: State is **creation of man**, not divine
- State should help man realise **good life**
- Concept of **Service State**

---

## Theory of Limited State

- Govt has **delegated powers**, not original powers
- Against absolute state (Plato, Hobbes, Rousseau)
- Constitutional & limited govt (*Second Treatise*)

---

## View of Human Nature

- Man has reason + passion (balanced)
- Even in state of nature, man reasonable
- Inspired M.N. Roy (morality for harmonious relations)

---

## Tabula Rasa

- Mind = clean slate
- Knowledge from experience

---

## Religious Toleration

- State should tolerate all religions
- Magistrate has no authority in religion

---

## Social Contract

- First contract → Civil Society
- Second contract → Civil Government

### Rights transferred to state:

1. Make law  
2. Execute law  
3. Adjudicate law  

Rest rights remain with people  
Government is a **trust**

---

## Natural Rights

- Life  
- Liberty  
- Property  

Inalienable, state must protect

---

## State Creation

- Not necessity, but convenience

---

## Theory of Consent

- Govt must rule by consent
- Laws based on people’s consent

---

## Right to Revolt

- People can revolt against unjust govt

---

## Locke on Property

- Sole purpose of Commonwealth = protection of property
- Scholar of possessive individualism
- Property = product of labour
- Labour part of personality

### Three Limits

1. Product of labour  
2. Equal right to property  
3. Property must not be spoiled  

---

## Criticism

- Marxists → vulgar defence of property

---

## Conclusion

Influenced **Hannah Arendt** and **John Rawls**
