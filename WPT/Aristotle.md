# Aristotle

---

## Intro

- Work: *Politics*
- Father of Political Science
- First to analyse politics **critically and systematically**
- Identified core elements: sovereignty of law, constitutionalism

---

## Plato & Aristotle — Similarities

- Both believed in **natural inequality**
  - Plato → Myth of metals  
  - Aristotle → Justification of slavery  
- State is prior to individual  
- No faith in masses  
  - Plato → Philosopher King  
  - Aristotle → Democracy second worst form  

---

## Plato vs Aristotle — Differences

- Plato: Ideas are reality, rejects world of matter  
- Aristotle: Accepts reality of this world; no ideal outside world  
- Plato: Specialised education  
- Aristotle: Common sense sufficient  
- Plato: Ruler must know ideas  
- Aristotle: Ruler must be worldly wise  

---

## Theory of Justice

- Given in context of **theory of revolution**
- Inherent inequality between men → justifies slavery  
- Treat equals equally, unequals unequally  
- **Proportionate equality**, not absolute equality  
- Those contributing more deserve more  
- Numerical equality relevant in democracy  
- Equality among unequals causes revolution  

---

## Theory of State

- “Man is by nature a political animal”
- State is natural habitat  
- State highest association  
- One who lives without state = beast or god  
- Man becomes man in presence of state  
- State more important than individual  

---

## Theory of Slavery

- Slavery natural and useful  
- Some lack reason/courage  
- Utility:
  - Master free for state duties  
  - Slave needs guidance  
  - Slave may develop virtue  
  - Slave can be freed if virtuous  

### Criticism

- “What is” ≠ “What ought to be”  
- Against Kant’s categorical imperative  

---

## Theory of Property

- Critique of Plato’s communism  
- Individual property, common use  
- Property gives achievement  
- Inspired Locke  
- Extreme wealth → revolution  
- Cite Thomas Piketty & inequality  

---

## Theory of Constitution

- Studied **158 constitutions**
- Classification:

| Number Rule | Good Form | Perverted Form |
|------------|-----------|----------------|
| One | Monarchy | Tyranny |
| Few | Aristocracy | Oligarchy |
| Many | Polity | Democracy |

---

## Polity

- Golden mean of democracy & oligarchy  
- Rule of middle class  

### Why Middle Class Rule

- Neither rich nor poor  
- Rich arrogant; poor ignorant  
- Both break law  
- Middle class ensures stability  
- Too much inequality → revolution  

---

## Theory of Revolution

- Conservative; anti-revolution  
- Any change = revolution  
- Main cause = inequality & injustice  
- Societies of extreme inequality collapse  

---

## Justice & Inequality

- Proportionate equality avoids injustice  
- Reward based on merit  
- State must recognise talent
