# Liberalism

---

## Intro

Liberalism is a political doctrine that takes protecting and enhancing **individual freedom** as the central theme of politics.

Chronology → Classical → Modern → Neo → Social Liberal

---

## Classical Liberalism

- Ideology of Enlightenment era  
- All are equal because all have **reason** — Locke  
- Man is **atomistic**  
- Equality in political sphere, not socio-economic  
- Man is supreme  

### Core Features

- Humanism central  
- Society is contractual, not natural  
- Society = aggregate of individual interests (market model)  
- Reject collective view of society  
- State = necessary evil  
- “That state is best which governs least” — Thomas Jefferson  
- Proportionate equality (vs socialist absolute equality)  
- Negative liberty  
- Against welfarism & progressive taxation  
- Sumner → drunkard in gutter is where he belongs  

### Bentham & Mill

- Bentham made liberalism utilitarian  
- Mill → liberty, not utility, is core value  

---

## Modern Liberalism / Welfare State

- Brings idealism into liberalism  
- Mill → liberty core theme  
- Man is social, has obligations  
- Developmental view of man  
- Reject atomistic view  

### Society & State

- Society is organic  
- State = instrument for happiness  
- T.H. Green → state source of virtue  
- State removes hindrances to liberty  
- Supports affirmative action  
- Golden mean between socialism & classical liberalism  
- Positive liberty  

---

## Kant & T.H. Green

- Kant → Human dignity  
- Green → Liberty = capacity to do what is worth doing  
- “Human consciousness postulates liberty; liberty demands rights; rights demand state.”

---

## Criticism

### Asian Values — Mahathir Mohammed

- Community over individual  
- Rights not absolute  
- Against UDHR  
- Cultural particularism  

### Left Critique

- Liberal society fails to remove racism, sexism  

---

## Liberalism and its Discontents — Fukuyama

- Liberalism doesn’t tell how to live  
- Moral vacuum filled by consumerism  

---

## Neoliberalism / Libertarianism

- Rolling back state  
- Revival of nightwatchman state  
- Market leads to Pareto optimal outcome  
- State became nanny state  
- Market > government  

### Advocates

1. Margaret Thatcher — TINA  
2. Ronald Reagan  
3. Milton Friedman — capitalism = precondition for freedom  
4. Robert Nozick — minimal state  
5. F.A. Hayek — planning = road to serfdom; social justice is mirage  
6. Murray Rothbard — politicians are band of thugs  

---

## Criticism of Neoliberalism

- Karl Polanyi → Society subordinated to market  
- Arun Maira → Liberal economics creates illiberal societies  
- Inequality → authoritarianism  

---

## Social Liberals / Liberal Egalitarians

- Humane governance  
- Emphasise human dignity  
- Capacity building & positive liberty  
- Post-WW2 concept  
- Civil society accountability  
- Inclusive growth  
- Community good harmonious with individual freedom  

### Thinkers

- Joseph Stiglitz → Asymmetric information; democratic deficit  
- Amartya Sen → Constructive role of state; capacity building  
- Dworkin  
- Rawls  

---

## Isaiah Berlin

**Book:** Two Concepts of Liberty  

- Values are plural  
- Negative liberty → Hayek, Nozick, Mill  
- Positive liberty → Green, Rousseau  
- Liberalism’s core value = toleration  
- Globalisation → need for toleration  
- Prefers negative liberty  
- Bird analogy  

---

## Conclusion

Liberalism evolved with time, emphasising individual freedom, limiting state authority, and promoting humanism and rationalism.
