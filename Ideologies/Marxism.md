# Marxism

---

## Intro

Marxism is a social, political, and economic philosophy named after **Karl Marx**, examining the effect of capitalism on labour, productivity and development, and arguing for a **worker revolution** to replace capitalism with communism.

---

## Types of Marxism

### Orthodox Marxism
- Economy = basic structure  

### Neo-Marxism
- Inspired from **Young Marx**  
- Focus on liberation from **alienation**

---

## Critical School (Frankfurt School)

- Critical of orthodox Marxism → led to totalitarian state  
- Marx’s aim = human liberation, end of alienation, freedom  
- Critical of liberal democracies → false consciousness  
- Critical of science → tool of capitalism  
- Modification → **Culture is basic structure**  
- Capitalism survives through advertising, ICT  
- Liberation difficult as capitalism became “common sense”

### Thinkers

#### Herbert Marcuse
- Father of **New Left**  
- Concept: **One-Dimensional Man** → mindless consumer society  

#### Theodor Adorno
- Negative dialectics  
- Society moving towards less freedom  

#### Max Horkheimer

## Structural School

- Multi-structural approach  
- State operates via:
  - Ideological State Apparatus (media, education)  
  - Coercive State Apparatus (police, army)

### Exponent

**Louis Althusser**
- Inspired by mature Marx  
- Differs from critical Marxists

## E.F. Schumacher — *Small is Beautiful*

1. Marxian analysis of modern industries  
2. Capitalism → dehumanisation (alienation)  
3. Work loses satisfaction; worker = cog  
4. Decisions based on profit, not human need  
5. Advocates people-centric economy  
6. Gigantism over small  
7. Happiness not from material wealth  
8. Capitalism → depression
