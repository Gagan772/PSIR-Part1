# Socialism

---

## Intro

Joseph A. Schumpeter — *Capitalism, Socialism and Democracy*  
Socialism = organisation of society where **means of production are controlled by public authority**, not private firms.

- Emerged against inequalities of Industrial Revolution  
- Primarily an economic doctrine  
- Rejects aggregative view of society; supports organic view  
- Core concept: **socio-economic equality**  
- Talks about **equality of outcomes** vs liberal proportionate equality  

---

## After Marx — Democratic/Evolutionary Socialism

### Edward Bernstein
- No need for violent revolution  
- Workers’ voting rights allow change via parliament  
- CPI India adopted parliamentary method  

### Karl Kautsky
- Democracy + socialism possible and logical  
- Replace class conflict → class cooperation  
- Replace dictatorship of proletariat → democracy  
- Revolutionary method → evolutionary method  

### Criticism

- Many varieties → lack clarity  
- Safety valve for capitalism  
- Fabian socialism based on middle-class intellectuals, not working class  
- Failed to mobilise working class  

### Indian Context

- MGNREGA → right to work  
- PMAY, Ujjwala → poverty reduction  

### German Social Democracy
- Parliamentary route to socialism  

---

## Fabian Socialism

- British variety  
- Named after Roman General Fabius  
- Gradual, peaceful socialism  
- Socialism comes when it becomes common sense  
- Middle-class intellectuals spread socialism  

**Thinkers**  
- G.D.H. Cole  
- Annie Besant  
- G.B. Shaw  

---

## Syndicalism

- French variety  
- Violent form  
- Nearest to Marxism  
- Goal: end political state; economic state run by workers  
- Workers become ruling class  

**Thinkers**  
- Emile Pouget  
- Sorel  

---

## Guild Socialism

- British variety  
- Exponent: G.D.H. Cole  
- Public ownership via guilds (economic associations)  
- Functional representation  
- Functional parliament (doctors represent doctors, etc.)  
- Combination of Fabianism + Syndicalism
