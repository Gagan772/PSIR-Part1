# Feminism

---

## Intro

- JS Mill — *The Subjugation of Women*  
  All slavery abolished but slavery of women continues; discrimination appears natural.

- Feminism = belief that women should have same rights and opportunities as men (Virginia Woolf)

- It is a **meta-ideology** (internal debates)

---

## Waves of Feminism

### First Wave

- Did not recognise difference between sex and gender  
- Focus: civil & political rights  

**Liberal Feminists**

1. **Mary Wollstonecraft** — Mother of feminism; demanded women’s voting rights (French Revolution)  
2. **Emmeline Pankhurst**, Elizabeth & Sylvia — Suffragette movement  

---

### Second Wave — Radical Feminism (True Feminism)

- Differentiates sex and gender  
- Central concept: **Patriarchy**  
- Calls for sexual revolution  
- “Personal is political” — Carole Hanisch  

#### Key Ideas

- Private sphere shapes public life  
- Gender division of labour in family limits women  
- PRI reservations increased women’s intra-household bargaining power (World Bank study)  
- Bihar alcohol ban linked to domestic violence  
- Rejects essentialism  

---

## Key Thinkers

### Susan Moller Okin — *Gender, Justice and Family*

- Family perpetuates gender inequality  
- Children internalise sexist values  

---

### Catherine MacKinnon — *Feminism Unmodified*

- Gender = system of dominance  
- State laws perpetuate patriarchy  
- Not destiny but customs oppress women  
- Myths institutionalise inequality  
- Women socialised into “feminine” traits  

---

### Simone de Beauvoir — *The Second Sex*

- Women = secondary sex  
- “One is not born woman, one becomes woman.”  
- Women exist as extension of men  

---

### Betty Friedan — *The Feminine Mystique*

- “Problem with no name”  
- Patriarchy = root problem  

#### Patriarchy Perpetuated Through

1. Family (core institution)  
2. Myths & customs (religion, marriage age, property denial)  
3. Knowledge systems (Freud example)  
4. Without ending patriarchy, rights cannot uplift women

## Globalisation and Women

### Negative Effects

- Beauty standards pressure  
- Increased gender inequality in third world  
- Loss of traditional jobs → informal sector marginalisation  
- Occupational hazards  
- Privatisation of health → denial of care  
- Male migration → feminisation of agriculture  

### Positive Effects

- Promotes liberal thought  
- Education & work opportunities  
- Women gain autonomy  

---

## Conclusion

Feminism is not about making women strong — women are already strong; it is about changing how the world perceives that strength.
