# Fascism

---

## Intro

- Derived from **Fascio** → bundle of rods (discipline, unity, strength)  
- WWII meaning → those bound to “live and die for the good of Italy”  
- First Fascio set up under **Mussolini**  
- 20th century = age of **authority, collectivism, totalitarian state**  
- Not systematic ideology; mix of ideas  

---

## Intellectual Inspirations

- **Plato** → Philosopher king; obedience; duties not rights  
- **Machiavelli** → National interest (raison d’état)  
- **Hegel** → State = March of God on Earth  
- **Hobbes** → Strong Leviathan to prevent anarchy  
- **Rousseau** → “Man must be forced to be free”  
- **Henri Bergson** → Vitality; emotion over reason  

---

## Causes of Fascism

1. **Ortega y Gasset** — *Revolt of the Masses*  
   Democracy without democratic culture → mobocracy → fascism  

2. **Ernst Nolte**  
   Democracy after WWI without culture  

3. **Erich Fromm** — Fear of Freedom  
   People prefer order & security over freedom  

4. **Amartya Sen**  
   People prefer authoritarianism if it provides basic needs  

---

## Features of Fascism

1. **Anti-rationalism** — Emotion, myths, propaganda  
2. **Totalitarianism** — Single party control of state & society  
3. No rights/liberties; censorship  
4. Rule by elite **junta**  
5. Protects capitalism; protectionism  
6. Anti-democracy — Parliament as “talking shop”  
7. Elitism & patriarchy  
8. Militarism; hierarchy  
9. Ultranationalism & imperialism  
10. Expansionism; geopolitics  
11. Autarky (self-sufficiency)  
12. “Nothing above the state…” — Mussolini  

---

## Criticism

1. **Arendt & Popper** → Form of totalitarianism; rise of mass politics  
2. **Gandhi** → Manifestation of Machiavellianism; immoral means  
3. **Laski** → Crisis of capitalism; counter-revolution; elite defence  

---

## Contemporary Relevance

- Fascists defeated in war, not rejected ideologically  
- Post 9/11 & 2008 crisis → rise of right-wing & neo-fascism  
- Crisis of liberal project

## Conclusion

**G.H. Sabine** — Fascism lacks consistency; hodgepodge ideology
